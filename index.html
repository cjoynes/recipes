Link with slight modifications.
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 1 green bell pepper
  • 1 jalapeno pepper (ribs and seeds removed, spiciness doesn't work with these flavors)
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cans black beans, drained
  • 2 tsp oregano
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp salt (+ to taste)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Rice
  • Lime
  1. Mince onion, bell pepper, jalapeno, garlic.
  2. Melt butter with olive oil in skillet over medium heat.
  3. Once melted, add onion, peppers, garlic. Saute ~10 minutes, until onions lightly browned and translucent.
  4. Add beans, cumin, oregano, salt, 1/2 cup water. Saute for ~15 more minutes (actually a lot shorter in practice, maybe 7-10 mins).
  5. Serve with rice, squeeze lime over. Add cilantro if you have it on hand.
Added May 2. Slightly modified from Cassie's recipe, think baked ziti with shells.
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 tsp garlic salt
  • 1 jar pasta sauce
  • 1 can stewed tomatoes, diced (~1 lb)
  • 1 box medium shell pasta
  • Ricotta
  • Provolone cheese, shredded or sliced
  • Mozzarella cheese, shredded
  1. Brown ground beef. Add onions, garlic salt, pasta sauce, diced stewed tomatoes. Mix well, simmer for 20 minutes.
  2. Cook pasta shells according to package. Drain and rinse with cold water (to stop cooking).
  3. Pour half the shells into a casserole dish, cover with half the meat+sauce mixture. Add a layer of ricotta, and cover with layer of provolone cheese. Repeat a layer of shells, sauce, and then a layer of mozzarella.
  4. Cover with foil and bake at 350F (175C) for 35-40 minutes. Uncover and continue baking until mozzarella browns and top shells are slightly crisp (about 5 minutes).

  • 2-1/4 cups warm water (110째 to 115째)
  • 3 tablespoons sugar plus 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons canola oil
  • 6-1/4 to 6-3/4 cups bread flour
  1. In a large bowl, dissolve yeast and 1/2 teaspoon sugar in warm water; let stand until bubbles form on surface. Whisk together remaining 3 tablespoons sugar, salt, and 3 cups flour.
  2. Stir oil into yeast mixture; pour into flour mixture and beat until smooth. Stir in enough remaining flour, 1/2 cup at a time, to form a soft dough.
  3. Turn onto a floured surface; knead until smooth and elastic, 8-10 minutes. Place in a greased bowl, turning once to grease the top. Cover and let rise in a warm place until doubled, 1-1/2 to 2 hours.
  4. Punch dough down. Turn onto a lightly floured surface; divide dough in half. Shape each into a loaf.
  5. Place in 2 greased 9x5-in. loaf pans. Cover and let rise until doubled, 1 to 1-1/2 hours.

Literally straight from the Nestle chocolate chip cookie bag (Nestle is evil tho fr fr)
  • 2 1/4 cups flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup softened butter (2 sticks)
  • 3/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cups packed brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cups chocolate chips (12 oz)
  1. Mix flour, baking soda, salt in bowl.
  2. Beat butter, sugar, brown sugar, and vanilla in large bowl until creamy. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Gradually beat in flour mixture.
  3. Stir in chocolate chips.
  4. Preheat oven to 375 F. Bake for 9-11 mins. (When I did it it actually took 13 mins, but the cookies were slightly larger)
From here.

Dough:

  • 3 cups flour (Maybe a bit less, 2.5 cups)
  • 1/2 cup milk (or water)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1+1/8 tsp salt
  • 10 g oil (look at the label for volume)
  1. Beat milk, eggs together. Add flour, salt, oil. Mix until shaggy dough forms.
  2. Let rest 10 minutes.
  3. Knead until smooth and passes window test. Keep refrigerated until forming dumplings.

Filling:

This ended up making a little more filling than necessary.
  • 2 lbs potatoes
  • Salt for boiling water
  • Cheddar cheese, 100g ish but probably more
  • 1 tbsp butter
  1. Peel and cube potatoes. Add to pot with salted water, bring to light boil.
  2. Stir pot, boil until potatoes are mashable with a fork.
  3. Drain potatoes, add butter and mash with a fork. Add cheese and keep mashing until smooth.

Forming:

  1. Roll dough out very thin. Thinner than you think.
  2. Use circular object to cut out circles the size you want your pierogi.
  3. Add filling to each, trying to keep on one half of the circle and leaving a rim.
  4. Pull the other half over the filling, sealing the edge and pressing a fork in if you want to be fancy.
  5. Boil salted water. Drop pierogi in, and remove once they float.
  6. Once most of the water has come off, add to pan with butter / olive oil and pan fry.
  7. Saute some yellow onions until soft, they go really well with the pierogi.
