Added May 2. Slightly modified from Cassie's recipe, think baked ziti with shells.
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 tsp garlic salt
  • 1 jar pasta sauce
  • 1 can stewed tomatoes, diced (~1 lb)
  • 1 box medium shell pasta
  • Ricotta
  • Provolone cheese, shredded or sliced
  • Mozzarella cheese, shredded
  1. Brown ground beef. Add onions, garlic salt, pasta sauce, diced stewed tomatoes. Mix well, simmer for 20 minutes.
  2. Cook pasta shells according to package. Drain and rinse with cold water (to stop cooking).
  3. Pour half the shells into a casserole dish, cover with half the meat+sauce mixture. Add a layer of ricotta, and cover with layer of provolone cheese. Repeat a layer of shells, sauce, and then a layer of mozzarella.
  4. Cover with foil and bake at 350F (175C) for 35-40 minutes. Uncover and continue baking until mozzarella browns and top shells are slightly crisp (about 5 minutes).

Chocolate Coating

Spinach pasta with cream sauce. Typically make with chicken on the side.
  • 1 box pasta (Gemelli if you can find it)
  • Olive oil
  • 4 tbsp butter (yes this is a lot)
  • Spinach. 1 bag / 1/2 box (the more the merrier)
  • ~4 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 tsp sage
  • 4-8 oz whipping cream / light cream
  • 1/2 cup Pecorino Romano cheese (can use parmesan as well)
  1. Get pasta water boiling, cook pasta while making sauce. You'll figure out the timing once you make it.
  2. Add olive oil, melt butter in pan on medium low. Once melted, add spinach and saute until wilted.
  3. Add garlic and saute until fragrant. Add sage, cream, cheese. Stir to prevent burning or skin formation. Add more cheese to taste.
  4. Once pasta and sauce are complete, combine in the pasta pot. This doesn't need thickening with pasta water, the cheese thickens the sauce. If it doesn't, add more cheese. You cannot have enough cheese in this recpie, I cannot stress this enough.
For Hatch green chiles. A New Mexico classic.
  • 1-2 Tbsp lard
  • 1.5 - 2 lbs pork shoulder / butt, or beef equivalent, cut into 1" cubes
  • 1 onion
  • A few cloves of garlic
  • 6 cups chicken or beef broth
  • 2 lbs potatoes, chopped.
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 cups chopped Hatch green chiles
  • 1 diced red bell pepper
  • Chopped cilantro (optional)
  1. Melt the lard in a pot, brown the pork. Then set aside.
  2. Saute the onion until translucent. Add garlic and saute.
  3. Return meat to pan. Add the rest of the ingredients, minus the chiles. Simmer for 1 hour.
  4. Add the chiles. Simmer another 20 mins or so.

  • 1/2 tsp salt

This can be done with any fat that is solid at room temperature.

Dough:

  • 450 g flour
  • 80 lard (can do less)
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 250 mL / g water
  • 5 g kosher salt
  1. Add flour, lard, baking powder to bowl. Use hands to pinch the lard through fingers to mix with the flour until a pebbly consistency is reached.
  2. Heat up water in microwave. Dissolve salt into water, then gradually add to flour mixture, stirring as you go.
  3. Once a dough ball forms, knead for about 5 mins. Dough should end up fairly smooth, not sticky.
  4. Divide dough into 60g portions. Roll into balls, then cover and let sit for 15 mins at room temperature.

Cooking:

  1. Preheat cast-iron skillet. Heat level will vary depending on stove power and how quickly you go.
  2. Roll out each ball as thin as you can make it (or use a tortilla press).
  3. One at a time, cook the tortillas. Time / side varies, wait until there are bubbles and browning on both sides.
