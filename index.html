Link with slight modifications.
  • 1 onion (usually sweet)
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 2-3 jalapeno peppers (remove biggest parts of ribs, but leave the rest)
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • olive oil to coat pan
  • 2 cans black beans, drained
  • 2 tsp oregano
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp salt (+ to taste)
  • water to add with beans
  • Rice
  • Lime
  1. Mince onion, bell pepper, jalapeno, garlic.
  2. Melt butter with olive oil in skillet over medium heat.
  3. Once melted, add onion, peppers, garlic. Saute ~10 minutes, until onions lightly browned and translucent.
  4. Add beans, cumin, oregano, salt, 1/2 cup water. Saute for ~15 more minutes (actually a lot shorter in practice, maybe 7-10 mins).
  5. Serve with rice, squeeze lime over. Add cilantro if you have it on hand.
Added May 2. Slightly modified from Cassie's recipe, think baked ziti with shells.
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 tsp garlic salt
  • 1 jar pasta sauce
  • 1 can stewed tomatoes, diced (~1 lb)
  • 1 box medium shell pasta
  • Ricotta
  • Provolone cheese, shredded or sliced
  • Mozzarella cheese, shredded
  1. Brown ground beef. Add onions, garlic salt, pasta sauce, diced stewed tomatoes. Mix well, simmer for 20 minutes.
  2. Cook pasta shells according to package. Drain and rinse with cold water (to stop cooking).
  3. Pour half the shells into a casserole dish, cover with half the meat+sauce mixture. Add a layer of ricotta, and cover with layer of provolone cheese. Repeat a layer of shells, sauce, and then a layer of mozzarella.
  4. Cover with foil and bake at 350F (175C) for 35-40 minutes. Uncover and continue baking until mozzarella browns and top shells are slightly crisp (about 5 minutes).

  2. Stir oil into yeast mixture; pour into flour mixture and beat until smooth. Stir in enough remaining flour, 1/2 cup at a time, to form a soft dough.

Spinach pasta with cream sauce. Typically make with chicken on the side.
  • 1 box pasta (Gemelli if you can find it)
  • Olive oil
  • 4 tbsp butter (yes this is a lot)
  • Spinach. 1 bag / 1/2 box (the more the merrier)
  • ~4 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 tsp sage
  • 4-8 oz whipping cream / light cream
  • 1/2 cup Pecorino Romano cheese (can use parmesan as well)
  1. Get pasta water boiling, cook pasta while making sauce. You'll figure out the timing once you make it.
  2. Add olive oil, melt butter in pan on medium low. Once melted, add spinach and saute until wilted.
  3. Add garlic and saute until fragrant. Add sage, cream, cheese. Stir to prevent burning or skin formation. Add more cheese to taste.
  4. Once pasta and sauce are complete, combine in the pasta pot. This doesn't need thickening with pasta water, the cheese thickens the sauce. If it doesn't, add more cheese. You cannot have enough cheese in this recpie, I cannot stress this enough.

From here.

Dough:

  • 3 cups flour (Maybe a bit less, 2.5 cups)
  • 1/2 cup milk (or water)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1+1/8 tsp salt
  • 10 g oil (look at the label for volume)
  1. Beat milk, eggs together. Add flour, salt, oil. Mix until shaggy dough forms.
  2. Let rest 10 minutes.
  3. Knead until smooth and passes window test. Keep refrigerated until forming dumplings.

Filling:

This ended up making a little more filling than necessary.
  • 2 lbs potatoes
  • Salt for boiling water
  • Cheddar cheese, 100g ish but probably more
  • 1 tbsp butter
  1. Peel and cube potatoes. Add to pot with salted water, bring to light boil.
  2. Stir pot, boil until potatoes are mashable with a fork.
  3. Drain potatoes, add butter and mash with a fork. Add cheese and keep mashing until smooth.

Forming:

  1. Roll dough out very thin. Thinner than you think.
  2. Use circular object to cut out circles the size you want your pierogi.
  3. Add filling to each, trying to keep on one half of the circle and leaving a rim.
  4. Pull the other half over the filling, sealing the edge and pressing a fork in if you want to be fancy.
  5. Boil salted water. Drop pierogi in, and remove once they float.
  6. Once most of the water has come off, add to pan with butter / olive oil and pan fry.
  7. Saute some yellow onions until soft, they go really well with the pierogi.

Dough

  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1/4 tsp yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup flour + more if necessary
  1. Combine water, yeast, and sugar. Let bloom ~5min to ensure yeast is active.
  2. Add salt and flour and mix until wet dough is formed. Knead until it can be stretched without tearing.
  3. Let proof for 1.5-2 hours at room temperature or 12-24 hours in refrigerator.

Cooking

Prepare toppings ahead of time, the cooking goes very fast.
  • 1/3 cup sauce
  • Whole milk low moisture mozzarella
  • Parmesan
  • Toppings
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Oregano
  1. Coat pan in a thin layer of olive oil, with a dash of salt, pepper, and oregano. Cornmeal can be used to aid with sticking.
  2. Stretch out dough into a round the size of cast iron. You can use gravity to help you. Let proof in the pan again for about 30 mins.
  3. Start broiler on high and burner on medium with cast iron on burner. Spread sauce across pizza.
  4. Five minutes after starting heat, add a sprinkling of parmesan and the mozzarella, then toppings.
  5. About 7-8 minutes after starting heat, place pan under broiler. Once browned to desired level (4-5 mins), remove from broiler.
  6. Let cool for a few minutes, loosen sides with butter knife, then move to plate.
This can be done with any fat that is solid at room temperature.

Dough:

  • 450 g flour
  • 80 lard / shortening (can do a bit more if wanted)
  • 5 g kosher salt
  • 250 mL / g water
  1. Add flour, lard to bowl. Use hands to pinch the lard through fingers to mix with the flour until a pebbly consistency is reached.
  2. Heat up water in microwave. Dissolve salt into water, then gradually add to flour mixture, stirring as you go.
  3. Once a dough ball forms, knead for about 5 mins. Dough should end up fairly smooth, not sticky.
  4. Divide dough into 60g portions. Roll into balls, then cover and let sit for 15 mins at room temperature.

Cooking:

  1. Preheat cast-iron skillet. Heat level will vary depending on stove power and how quickly you go.
  2. Roll out each ball as thin as you can make it, then dust with flour to keep from sticking to the stack of uncooked tortillas.
  3. One at a time, cook the tortillas. Time / side varies, wait until there are bubbles and browning on both sides.
